About

My life is more than just a job, it is about the experience of helping others.

  • January 2014

    My jump into technology

    I jump-started my career at Google where I worked to support the Maps teams by deploy free open-source resource monitoring solutions across 3 countries and 4 offices. The intent was to help us determine what needed to be replaced when doing upgrades on the computers. The Zabbix deployment resulted in saving $2Million on machine replacement.

  • June 2015

    Becoming a Founder and DevOps Specialist

    Arriving at F5 Networks I joined the Virtual Edition BigIP Network Load Balancer Test Automation Team working to build a lot of network traffic related test scripts. Eventually We were charged with the push into cloud services. I was tasked to do the cloud service provider evaluation for over 30 cloud service providers. This is where I fell in love with AWS. This Dropped me out of coding wich was my first love in Tech and I started to build Tourzan.com to keep me fresh while I learned this new career path called DevOps.

  • November 2015

    Tourzan.com

    While continuing to work for F5 Networks I built up Tourzan.com from zero locations to over 400 cities served. My company allowed me outlet my passion for software by building production grade API's, drop in UI's, Understand how other business models work like AirBnB, Uber and more. I was able to build something on my other passion, International Travel. Sales and marketing became very difficult for me to Manage until I met a business partner who helped me realize we all need help sometimes. Tourzan runs entirely as a remote entity within AWS and has been hand built by a team I put together and manage. Sadly, It still did't not make enough for me to quit my day job.

  • June 2018

    PACCAR and Self Driving Technology

    Working for PACCAR has been a nice break from the tech industry vibe. I was put on a few really cool projects that have my interest. Notably a custom implementation with an F5 Load balancer and a custom script for our Tableau Cluster that saves the company over $400,000 annually. Though for the majority of my tenure here I was in charge of networking and data offload on their Self-Driving Semi Truck. My code contributions, local and global network configurations are part of a future global offering that will have a major impact on supply lines and more. This got my mind wondering about other parts of self-driving vehicles and I realized that not many in logistics were working on vehicle communication systems. After getting permission to persue this on my own I started Metrolla.com

  • November 2019

    Metrolla.com

    Metrolla.com allowed me to flex my muscles in network technology, let me use my experience with PACCAR, and give me more hands on experience with hardware and cloud based decentralized scalability. I decided I wanted the devices to be auto provisioning, hyper accurate, allow for streaming of other on board sensors, act as a redundant system for other sensors. Most importantly of all it needed to run on existing technology and be scalable on that technology. And it did all of that and more. I am working to build out the sales teams at this time to start getting partners to implement it.

  • August 2018

    Stratus10 and the birth of RoboNimbus.com

    In 2018 i was contacted by Oscar Moncada who with his co-founder run Stratus10.com a full shop for managed AWS. Oscar hired me to subcontract for them while they grew their business. I figured this would be a good opportunity for some extra fun money and for me to learn more about the management side of consulting work. This had me thinking about how many out there need managed services but really cant afford it because they are just too small.

Tourzan.com

Build a platform that allows locals to make side cash showing people around where they live.

The website started as a way for me to continue to code while going through a major change in my career. I decided to take my passion for travel and found that there was an entire segment lacking in the tourism industry for Airbnb like services. Tourzan.com filled that need. Tourzan features a full two sided web marketplace with global payouts to 220 countries, Secure integration API's for 3rd parties, white labeled drop in UI's for 3rd party integrations, a commissioned mobile app, global centralized distribution through my own AWS Architecture, real time chat system for communication, Full translation of the site in 6 different languages, and is servicing 112+ countries and 400+ Cities. It was my first forey into business and startups and i built it from nothing to where it is single handedly. It taught me a lot and it put me on the path I am today.

  • Date: November 2015

Metrolla.com

Give a voice to autonomous vehicles with existing tech.

After realizing the lack of communication services within the logistics segment of autonomous vehicles, I decided to challenge myself to build a SaaS for RnD Startups and Customers of Self driving delivery vehicles. The concept was simple, use existing widely implemented technology like sockets, REST API's and build a small form factor device that could piggy back on other services on the vehicle. While I was working on it, I realized this could also work as a tracking solution and alternate data hub. Eventually it became clear that the small device could be a simple backup and full service asset solution for large companies. With a few emails I managed to get myself test data from sensor providers and started building out the processes to stream Lidar and video from the vehicles right to my desktop. This solidified my platform as the only service that can do this.

  • Date: November 2019
  • Client: Metrolla.com
  • Category: Full service development and engineering
